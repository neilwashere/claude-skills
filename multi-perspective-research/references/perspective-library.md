# Perspective Library

A catalog of lenses to draw on when selecting perspectives in Step 1. This is a menu, not a
checklist — pick the **3–6** that are most adversarial and differently-grounded for the
specific topic. Each lens is defined by *what evidence it reads*, which is why different
lenses surface different facts.

The goal of selection is **maximum tension and coverage**: lenses that would just agree with
each other are redundant; include at least one whose explicit job is to argue the mainstream
is wrong.

## The classic five (good general-purpose fallback)

- **The Practitioner** — does this daily. Reads: field experience, what actually breaks in
  the real world. Surfaces the practical realities theory ignores.
- **The Academic** — studies it rigorously. Reads: peer-reviewed evidence, meta-analyses.
  Surfaces where the data contradicts popular belief.
- **The Skeptic / Contrarian** — thinks the consensus is wrong. Reads: the strongest
  counter-evidence, replication failures. Surfaces what proponents conveniently ignore.
- **The Economist** — follows the money. Reads: incentives, funding flows, market signals.
  Surfaces who profits from the prevailing narrative. *(Drop if there's no money in play.)*
- **The Historian** — has seen the pattern before. Reads: precedent, prior cycles. Surfaces
  what historical parallels predict about how this plays out.

## Domain-specific lenses to swap in

Reach for these when they're more generative than a classic slot:

- **The Regulator / Policymaker** — what's legal, mandated, or about to be. Reads statute,
  guidance, enforcement actions.
- **The Engineer / Builder** — how it actually gets implemented. Reads architecture,
  constraints, failure modes.
- **The Operator / SRE** — what it costs to *run* at scale. Reads incidents, toil, on-call
  reality.
- **The End User / Customer** — lived experience of the thing. Reads reviews, support
  tickets, ethnography.
- **The Clinician / Frontline Expert** — applies it to real cases under uncertainty.
- **The Bench Researcher** — mechanism and first principles, upstream of application.
- **The Finance / Procurement lens** — total cost, ROI, budget reality.
- **The Ethicist** — second-order harms, fairness, who bears the externalities.
- **The Investor / Market** — where capital is betting and why.
- **The Insider Whistleblower** — what the industry knows but doesn't say publicly.
- **The Adversary / Attacker** — how it gets gamed, exploited, or abused (essential for
  security, fraud, and integrity topics).
- **The Adjacent-Field Expert** — someone from a neighboring discipline who sees the problem
  through a different paradigm.

## Selection heuristics

- **Test each pair for friction.** If lens A and lens B would reach the same conclusion from
  the same evidence, you have one lens, not two. Cut the weaker.
- **Cover the evidence base.** Aim for lenses that read field data, formal research, market
  signals, and historical record — so the union covers more than any single search would.
- **Always include a true contrarian.** Without one, the consensus check in Step 3 is
  meaningless — agreement among lenses that were never going to disagree proves nothing.
- **Match the user's decision.** If they'll act as a buyer, include Procurement; as a
  builder, include Engineer/Operator; as a regulator, include Ethicist and Adversary.
